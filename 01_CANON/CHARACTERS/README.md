# Character Canon

This directory contains canonical character profiles for *The Invisible Boy*.

Character canon exists to preserve:
- psychological continuity
- behavioral consistency
- speech identity
- relationship continuity
- investigative coherence
- emotional authenticity

---

# Character Profile Standards

Each character file should contain, when applicable:

- Full name
- Aliases or known identifiers
- Approximate age
- Physical appearance
- Speech patterns
- Personality traits
- Psychological profile
- Relationship dynamics
- Known history
- Motivations
- Behavioral tendencies
- Timeline relevance
- Investigative relevance
- Restrictions or canon locks
- Continuity notes

Not every field is required for every character.

Profiles should remain concise, authoritative, and continuity-focused.

---

# Repository Rules

Character canon may evolve during development, but modifications require review.

Major revisions should undergo:
- continuity review
- reconciliation review if necessary
- author approval before LOCKED status

Locked character traits may not be altered without explicit approval.

---

# AI Interaction Rules

AI systems may:
- reference character canon
- validate continuity
- detect characterization drift
- compare dialogue consistency

AI systems may NOT:
- silently reinterpret character psychology
- mutate core personality traits automatically
- override established emotional continuity
- rewrite locked characterization

---

# Canon Priority

When conflicts occur:

1. LOCKED character canon
2. APPROVED character canon
3. VERIFIED continuity materials
4. Draft manuscripts
5. Experimental AI outputs

---

# Literary Principle

Character consistency is structural.

Character humanity is literary.

Verification systems may support continuity, but literary judgment remains human-authoritative.
